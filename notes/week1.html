First Principles AGI Dinner Notes

Week 1 - May 2025

Key Themes Discussed

AGI Capabilities and Representation

  • What does AGI enable?
    • Infinite conversations with something that has infinite knowledge
    • Important subtlety: tacit knowledge (LLMs don't know what Bob's pizza store made yesterday, or how I helped a squirrel today etc.)
  • For LLMs or DNA models, there is a unit of information. A smallest relevant unit:
    • For LLMs, it's a letter or a word
    • For DNA, it's a nucleotide
    • The relationships between these units hold meaning (a word's meaning is represented by other words)
  • What is the equivalent for physical reality? A box's meaning is not necessarily represented by other boxes
  • We have not yet figured out the right data representation of physical reality
  • What are the domains for which we do not yet have the correct data representations?

Diffusion Models and Physics

  • Beautiful explanation of diffusion models: there are things for which entropy increase is easy; the hard thing is to go backwards
  • As you add heat to a system, entropy increases - it gets more chaotic
  • It's extremely hard to run this process in reverse, but with some set of rules, we *can* work backwards
  • This lets us reach an interesting state with perhaps novel properties (representing a different state from which we could have come)
  • Loop concept: physics making biology making humans understanding physics
  • TTT - test time training

Architecture and Representation

  • Question: Why are all architects drawing on paper?
  • They show sketches of the outside of a house, but lack of digitally represented architecture prevents modeling things like weather (e.g., the puddle example)
  • Side note: This also means limited training data for architecture foundation models

CAD and Physical Modeling

  • CAD happens in the space of user intuition, relying heavily on context that sits in the user's imagination
  • "If I have to write out all 672 dimensions, I might as well just CAD the whole thing"
  • Counter: couldn't you compress the number of dimensions you have to write out in your language?
  • Point: you can't tokenize operations like extrude
  • Counter: In code, the word "for" signals a loop operation. Why can't you say the same thing in the CAD case?
  • Question about assembly code
  • Response: Models are trained on the high-level language - they're trained on the abstracted data

Automation and Future Jobs

  • High frequency, low variance tasks are already solved (e.g., assembly line doing *exact* same thing)
  • High frequency, high variance is more interesting and currently harder to automate (e.g., pick and place worker using different components with different geometries)
  • Concern about education, language learning and employability
  • English proficiency = social status + employability
  • Question: By the time we have sufficiently good voice language learning apps, won't jobs like call centers have been replaced?

Text vs. Physical Reality

  • Something interesting about text: its very nature demands recording it - because we read it online, it must be stored for reading
  • Physical reality is experienced in the real world - so its "data" is mostly stored in the real world and not online
  • Thought exercise: What is an efficient way to store data about physical objects?

Model Architecture

  • Do we need a fundamentally new model architecture?
  • Model architecture for physical reality is still a very active area of research

Future Ideas

  • Train a CAD model as an autocomplete
  • Use the user's prior history of "CAD history timelines" to suggest a next edit from their current position
